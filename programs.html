Junior High School

Special Program for the Arts

The Special Program for the Arts is a nationwide program for students with potentials or talents in the Arts. One pilot school in every region was selected to pilot and implement this program and is now on its tenth year of implementation. The selected school offers a comprehensive secondary education program centered in the Arts, covering a range of art forms and disciplines. Arts education is an integral component of a balanced educational program in all year levels which also provides the background for post-secondary/ tertiary level work. Special Program for Information Communication and Technology

The Special Program in Information and Communications Technology (SP-ICT) is an initiative program for elementary and junior high school students with ICT talents and skills in Nueva Ecija. The program is available at Santa Rosa National High School, one of four pioneer schools in the region. he Special Program in Information and Communications Technology (SP-ICT) is a program for students in elementary and junior high school who have skills and talents in Information and Communications Technology (ICT). The program is run by the Division of Nueva Ecija, which is part of the Department of Education. The Schools Division of Nueva Ecija started implementing the program in 2017.

Special Program for Journalism

The Special Program for Journalism (SPJ)is designed to improve student writers' journalistic abilities and competencies, enhance their experiences, and support independent and responsible journalism. Special journalism programs aim to equip participants with the necessary tools and knowledge to excel in the rapidly evolving media landscape. This includes training in research and reporting techniques, ethics and standards in journalism, digital storytelling platforms, media law and ethics, and critical thinking skills. Additionally, these programs often provide opportunities for networking, mentorship, and hands-on experience through internships or collaborative projects with industry professionals.

Science Technology and Engineering

In Special Science Classes (SSC), Science, Mathematics, Engineering, and Research are developed through the flagship program, Science, Technology, and Engineering (STE) Program. The nation's secondary schools follow the Science Curriculum Framework, which outlines the subjects' requirements, key competencies, methodologies, and guiding principles. The framework is built on the development of a functionally literate Filipino who can solve problems critically, think creatively, and make informed decisions in order to preserve the environment, conserve resources, and maintain a high standard of living. This individual must also demonstrate an understanding of fundamental science concepts and processes.

Regular Class

The Regular Class Program is a high school program that focuses on instructional activities that help students gain movement skills and concepts to help them function in their daily lives. The regular class program consists of continuity of movement experiences.

A regular school program is the classroom-based instructional program that a student or adult education student would have attended if they had not elected independent study. Regular classes are the classes to which a student has been assigned by the school where they are enrolled.

Senior High School

Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics

The Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics (STEM) strand is a senior high school program in the Philippines that focuses on research and inquiry, and exposes students to more complex science and math concepts. The STEM strand is intended to serve as a foundation for students' future college degrees Through various projects and research entailed, students develop problem-solving skills where they can apply scientific, technological, mathematical, and engineering concepts.

The ABM strand is designe for students interested in business, finance, entrepreneurship, and accounting. Hummanities and Social Sciences

Humanities and Social Science (HUMSS)Strand HUMSS stands for Humanities and Social Sciences, and it's one of the Senior High School (SHS) academic tracks. HUMSS is for students who want to study journalism, communication arts, liberal arts, education, and other social science-related courses in college. HUMSS courses cover a variety of fields that explore the human condition using analytical, critical, and empirical ways.

Technical and Vocational Livelihood

Technical-Vocational-Livelihood (TVL) track The SHS program has a Technical-Vocational-Livelihood (TVL) Track, which has four strands: Agri-Fishery Arts, Home Economics (HE), Information and Communication Technology (ICT), and Industrial Arts. These are aligned with Technology and Livelihood Education (TLE) in Grades 7-10. Each TVL strand offers various specializations that may or may not have a National Certificate (NC) equivalent from TESDA. The time allocation per strand specialization is based on TESDA Training Regulations-Based Courses and is only indicative since the standard time allotment of 80 hours per semester per subject will still be applied. Therefore, each strand specialization must be designed to fit into the 80-hour blocks of time. Technology Livelihood Education and Technical-Vocational Livelihood Track Subjects may be taken between Grades 9-12. Schools may offer specializations from the four strands as long as the minimum number of hours for each specialization is met. Curriculum Maps may then be modified according to specializations offered by a school. General Academic Strand

General Academic Strand (GAS)The fourth strand in the Academic Track is the General Academic Strand, which has subjects selected from the different strands. This strand is ideal for SHS students who have not yet decided on a particular specialization. This strand, which is also a viable offering in secondary schools with a low student population, allows for electives that may be a combination of related subjects from the other tracks or strands in the SHS curriculum.

The GAS is ideal for students who are undecided about their future career path. It can help you understand your role in the community and to be an upstanding citizen in your country. It can also help you develop a sense of social responsibility and encourage you to contribute to society for its growth and development.
